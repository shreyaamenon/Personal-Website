about me ✧

My interest in computer science began in high school when I enrolled in my first computer science course. Learning Java sparked a genuine fascination, as I found the process of writing functional code both engaging and rewarding. This initial exposure inspired me to pursue a degree in Computer Science at the university level.

During my internship at Amazon Web Services as a Cloud Support Associate, I gained hands-on experience working with services such as EC2, S3, and IAM. The fast-paced and high-pressure environment strengthened my problem-solving abilities, enhanced my communication skills, and taught me how to quickly identify and resolve critical issues.

I also served as a Mobile App Developer Assistant at Nerveli, where I contributed to the development of a health-focused mobile application using React Native. My responsibilities included designing user interface components, implementing authentication features, and assisting in the integration of an AI model to support symptom tracking functionality.

After graduating, I completed an intensive (almost) year-long AI and Machine Learning certification program through the University of Texas at Austin. As part of the program, I developed several projects, including a sales forecasting model built with Python and XGBoost, which I successfully deployed using Streamlit. This experience further deepened my passion for data science, artificial intelligence, and machine learning, while providing me with valuable exposure to a range of hands-on, end-to-end projects.
